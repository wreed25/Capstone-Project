# Predicting NFL Player Value and Contract overpayment Model

## The league is essentially a 32-team Enterprise operating under a hard financial ceiling. In 2026, each team has a $301.2 million salary cap, while contract structures can push costs into future years through signing-bonus proration and dead money. That makes every roster decision a blend of performance forecasting, finance, risk, and probability. Each team has to decide where to spend limited salary-cap dollars, which players to retain, who can replace an injured starter, and whether draft picks will produce more value than veterans.

## Preliminary Question

### Can machine learning help NFL front offices make better contract decisions by predicting a player's future value before signing or extending a contract?

## Hypothesis

### Wide receivers with strong recent efficiency, stable target share, high snap participation, younger age, and lower recent injury-related absence will produce more future value per salary-cap dollar than receivers whose contracts are primarily supported by past volume or a single unusually productive season.

## Prediction

### If the hypothesis is correct, the model will identify measurable overpayment risk among older receivers, players with declining efficiency, players with falling snap or target shares, and players whose recent production substantially exceeds their longer-term performance trend.
